Chapter 1 — Atomic Structure

Atomic structure explains the arrangement of subatomic particles inside an atom.

Discovery of Electron

J.J Thomson performed the cathode ray experiment in 1897 and discovered electrons.

  • Electron charge = -1.602 × 10⁻¹⁹ C
  • Mass = 9.11 × 10⁻³¹ kg
  • Located outside nucleus
Particle Discoverer Charge
Electron J.J Thomson Negative
Proton Goldstein/Rutherford Positive
Neutron James Chadwick Zero
AFNS High Yield:
Atomic number = number of protons
Mass number = proton + neutron

Chapter 2 — Atomic Models

Rutherford Atomic Model

  • Gold foil experiment
  • Discovery of nucleus
  • Most space inside atom is empty

Defects

  • Could not explain stability of atom
  • Could not explain line spectrum

Bohr Model

  • Electrons move in fixed energy levels
  • Energy absorbed/emitted during transition
  • Explains hydrogen spectrum
Bohr model works best for hydrogen-like atoms.

Max Planck proposed that energy is emitted in small packets called quanta.

Concept Scientist
Quantum Theory Max Planck

AFNS Focus: Energy of radiation depends on frequency.

Chapter 4 — Quantum Numbers

Quantum numbers describe the position and behaviour of electrons inside an atom.

Quantum Number Symbol Function
Principal n Shows shell / energy level
Azimuthal l Shows subshell
Magnetic m Shows orbital orientation
Spin s Electron spin direction

Subshell Capacity

  • s orbital → 2 electrons
  • p orbital → 6 electrons
  • d orbital → 10 electrons
  • f orbital → 14 electrons
AFNS Trap:
Maximum electrons in shell = 2n²

Chapter 5 — Electronic Configuration

Electronic configuration describes distribution of electrons in orbitals.

Important Rules

1. Aufbau Principle

Electrons occupy orbitals of lower energy first.

2. Pauli Exclusion Principle

No two electrons can have the same four quantum numbers.

3. Hund's Rule

Electrons fill degenerate orbitals singly before pairing.

Orbital Maximum Electrons
s 2
p 6
d 10
f 14
Important exceptions:
Cr = [Ar] 3d⁵ 4s¹
Cu = [Ar] 3d¹⁰ 4s¹

Chapter 6 — Chemical Bonding

Chemical bonding is the force that holds atoms together.

Types of Bonds

Bond Formation
Ionic Bond Transfer of electrons
Covalent Bond Sharing of electrons
Coordinate Bond One atom provides both electrons

VSEPR Theory

  • Electron pairs repel each other.
  • Molecules adopt shapes with minimum repulsion.
Molecule Shape
CO₂ Linear
NH₃ Trigonal pyramidal
H₂O Bent
AFNS Favorite:
Ionic compounds have high melting points.

Chapter 7 — Mole Concept & Stoichiometry

The mole connects microscopic particles with measurable quantities.

Quantity Value
1 mole 6.022 × 10²³ particles
Molar mass Mass of one mole

Important Formulas

  • Moles = Given mass / Molar mass
  • Particles = moles × Avogadro number
AFNS MCQ: Avogadro number = 6.022 × 10²³

Chapter 8 — States of Matter

Gas Laws

Law Relationship
Boyle Law Pressure inversely proportional to volume
Charles Law Volume directly proportional to temperature
Ideal Gas Equation PV=nRT

Kinetic Molecular Theory

  • Particles are always moving.
  • Collisions are elastic.
  • Temperature increases kinetic energy.
Remember: Higher temperature = faster molecular motion

Chapter 9 — Thermochemistry

Thermochemistry deals with heat changes during chemical reactions.

Important Terms

  • System: Part of universe under study.
  • Surroundings: Everything outside system.
  • Enthalpy (H): Heat content of a system.
Reaction Type Heat Change
Exothermic Heat released (ΔH negative)
Endothermic Heat absorbed (ΔH positive)

Hess Law

The total enthalpy change of a reaction is independent of the pathway.

AFNS Focus: Combustion reactions are usually exothermic.

Chapter 10 — Chemical Equilibrium

Equilibrium occurs when forward and reverse reactions happen at equal rates.

Le Chatelier Principle

A system at equilibrium shifts to oppose any applied change.

Change Effect
Increase concentration Shifts to reduce it
Increase pressure Favours fewer gas molecules
Increase temperature Depends on reaction heat

Equilibrium Constant

Kc shows relationship between reactants and products at equilibrium.

AFNS MCQ: Catalyst changes rate but does not change equilibrium position.

Chapter 11 — Acids, Bases and pH

Definitions

Theory Concept
Arrhenius Produces H⁺ or OH⁻ in water
Bronsted Lowry Proton donor/acceptor
Lewis Electron pair donor/acceptor

pH Scale

  • pH < 7 = Acidic
  • pH = 7 = Neutral
  • pH > 7 = Basic
Strong acids completely ionize in water.

Chapter 12 — Electrochemistry

Electrochemistry studies conversion between chemical and electrical energy.

Oxidation and Reduction

  • Oxidation = Loss of electrons
  • Reduction = Gain of electrons
Term Meaning
Anode Oxidation occurs
Cathode Reduction occurs
Remember: OIL RIG Oxidation Is Loss Reduction Is Gain
