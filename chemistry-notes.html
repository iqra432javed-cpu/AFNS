Chapter 1: Discovery of Electron, Proton and Neutron

  • Electron was discovered by J.J. Thomson using cathode ray experiments.
  • Electron carries negative charge and has very small mass.
  • Proton was identified through positive rays and carries positive charge.
  • Neutron was discovered by James Chadwick in 1932.
Particle Charge Location
Electron -1 Outside nucleus
Proton +1 Nucleus
Neutron 0 Nucleus
Quick Revision: Atomic number depends on number of protons while mass number equals protons plus neutrons.

Chapter 2: Cathode Rays & Canal Rays Properties

  • Cathode rays travel from cathode towards anode.
  • They consist of negatively charged electrons.
  • Canal rays are positive rays discovered by Goldstein.
  • Canal rays depend on gas present inside discharge tube.
Property Cathode Rays Canal Rays
Charge Negative Positive
Nature Electrons Positive ions
Cathode rays proved the existence of electrons.

Chapter 3: Discovery of Neutron (Chadwick's Experiment)

  • Chadwick bombarded beryllium with alpha particles.
  • Unknown radiation produced neutral particles.
  • These particles were named neutrons.
Scientist Discovery
Chadwick Neutron
Rutherford Nucleus
Neutron has no charge but contributes to atomic mass.

Chapter 4: Rutherford's Atomic Model & Defects

  • Gold foil experiment discovered the nucleus.
  • Atom contains a small dense positive nucleus.
  • Electrons revolve around nucleus.

Defects

  • Could not explain atomic stability.
  • Could not explain line spectra.
Rutherford model introduced the nuclear concept of atom.

Chapter 5: Bohr's Atomic Theory & Postulates

  • Electrons move in fixed energy levels.
  • Electrons absorb or emit energy during transitions.
  • Only certain orbits are allowed.
Shell Name
K n=1
L n=2
M n=3
Energy increases as distance from nucleus increases.

Chapter 6: Planck's Quantum Theory

  • Energy is emitted in small packets called quanta.
  • Energy of quantum depends on frequency.
  • This theory explained black body radiation.
Higher frequency radiation has higher energy.

Chapter 7: X-Rays & Atomic Number (Moseley's Law)

  • X-rays are produced when high speed electrons strike metals.
  • Moseley proved atomic number is fundamental property.
  • Modern periodic table is based on atomic number.

Chapter 8: Quantum Numbers

  • Principal quantum number (n) represents shell.
  • Azimuthal quantum number represents subshell.
  • Magnetic quantum number represents orbital orientation.
  • Spin quantum number represents electron spin.
Four quantum numbers completely describe an electron.

Chapter 9: Electronic Configuration Rules

  • Aufbau principle: electrons fill lower energy orbitals first.
  • Pauli exclusion principle: no two electrons have identical quantum numbers.
  • Hund's rule: orbitals fill singly before pairing.

Chapter 10: Modern Periodic Table & Shielding Effect

  • Elements are arranged according to atomic number.
  • Shielding effect reduces attraction between nucleus and electrons.
  • Effective nuclear charge controls many trends.

Chapter 11: Ionization Energy & Electron Affinity Trends

  • Ionization energy is energy required to remove electron.
  • Generally increases across a period.
  • Electron affinity shows tendency to accept electrons.

Chapter 12: Electronegativity & Metallic Character

  • Electronegativity is attraction for bonding electrons.
  • Non-metals usually have higher electronegativity.
  • Metallic character increases down groups.

Chapter 13: Atomic Radius & Ionic Radius Trends

  • Atomic radius decreases across a period.
  • Atomic radius increases down a group.
  • Cations are smaller than their atoms.
  • Anions are larger than their atoms.
Remember: Across period = size decreases. Down group = size increases.
