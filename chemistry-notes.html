Atomic Structure & Periodic Trends

AFNS Chemistry high-yield preparation material

Chapter 1 — Discovery of Electron, Proton and Neutron

Atoms contain three fundamental particles. Their discovery formed the foundation of modern atomic theory.

  • Electron discovered by J.J. Thomson through cathode rays.
  • Proton carries positive charge and exists inside nucleus.
  • Neutron was discovered by James Chadwick.
Particle Charge Position
Electron Negative Outside nucleus
Proton Positive Nucleus
Neutron Neutral Nucleus
Quick Revision: Atomic number = Number of protons
Mass number = Protons + Neutrons

Chapter 2 — Cathode Rays & Canal Rays

  • Cathode rays are streams of electrons.
  • They move from cathode toward anode.
  • Canal rays contain positive ions.
AFNS Tip: Cathode rays proved existence of electrons.

Chapter 3 — Chadwick's Neutron Discovery

  • Alpha particles were bombarded on beryllium.
  • Neutral radiation was produced.
  • These particles were called neutrons.

Chapter 4 — Rutherford Atomic Model

  • Gold foil experiment discovered nucleus.
  • Atom contains dense positive center.
  • Electrons revolve around nucleus.
Major defect: Could not explain atomic stability.

Chapter 5 — Bohr Atomic Theory

  • Electrons move in fixed energy levels.
  • Energy changes occur during transitions.
  • Each orbit has specific energy.

Chapter 6 — Planck Quantum Theory

  • Energy exists in small packets called quanta.
  • Energy depends on frequency.

Chapter 7 — X-Rays and Moseley's Law

  • X-rays are produced by fast electrons hitting metals.
  • Moseley established atomic number importance.

Chapter 8 — Quantum Numbers

  • Principal quantum number describes shell.
  • Azimuthal describes subshell.
  • Magnetic describes orbital orientation.
  • Spin describes electron rotation.

Chapter 9 — Electronic Configuration Rules

  • Aufbau: lower energy first.
  • Pauli: two electrons cannot have same quantum numbers.
  • Hund: single filling before pairing.

Chapter 10 — Modern Periodic Table

  • Elements arranged by atomic number.
  • Shielding reduces attraction.

Chapter 11 — Ionization Energy

  • Energy required to remove electron.
  • Generally increases across period.

Chapter 12 — Electronegativity

  • Ability to attract bonding electrons.
  • Non-metals have higher values.

Chapter 13 — Atomic and Ionic Radius

  • Atomic radius decreases across period.
  • Radius increases down group.
  • Cations are smaller than atoms.
Remember: Across = decrease | Down = increase
